Our Eco-Friendly Promise

Made from plant-based materials. Lower carbon footprint. A step towards a better planet.

The Material

What is PLA?

PLA (Polylactic Acid) is a bio-plastic made from plant-based materials like corn starch and sugarcane. Unlike traditional petroleum-based plastics, PLA is derived from plants rather than fossil fuels.

It's one of the most popular sustainable materials used in 3D printing today, and it's what we use to create all of our fidget toys.

When disposed of in industrial composting facilities, PLA can break down into natural elements. However, it's important to note that PLA requires specific industrial conditions to biodegrade ‚Äî it won't break down in home compost or landfill.

The Difference

PLA vs Traditional Plastics

Unlike petroleum-based plastics that take hundreds of years to decompose, PLA offers a sustainable alternative that's better for our environment.

Traditional Plastics

  • Made from petroleum (fossil fuels)
  • Takes 500-1000 years to decompose
  • Releases harmful chemicals
  • Non-renewable resource
  • Contributes to ocean pollution

PLA (What We Use)

  • Made from corn starch & sugarcane
  • Biodegradable in composting facilities
  • No toxic chemicals released
  • Plant-based materials
  • Lower carbon footprint
From Nature

Made from Plant-Based Materials

🌽

Corn Starch

The primary ingredient in PLA comes from the starches found in corn, a renewable crop that can be regrown each season.

🌿

Sugarcane

Sugarcane is another plant-based source used to create the lactic acid that forms the building blocks of PLA plastic.

🌱

Tapioca Roots

Cassava or tapioca roots provide starch that can also be converted into PLA, offering another sustainable source.

Our Impact

Why It Matters

Lower Carbon Footprint

Studies show PLA production generates significantly fewer greenhouse gases compared to traditional petroleum-based plastic manufacturing.

Plant-Based Origin

Unlike petroleum plastics, PLA is derived from plants like corn and sugarcane which can be regrown each season.

Non-Toxic & Safe

PLA doesn't release harmful chemicals during production or decomposition, making it safer for both people and the environment.

Industrial Composting

PLA can break down in industrial composting facilities, though proper disposal infrastructure is still developing in many areas.
